**Multiply-line proxy**: lines containing ` * ` across all module *definitions* in the file.
**Instance-weighted proxy**: same count but each module is multiplied by how many times it is instantiated.
For flat designs with no sub-module reuse the two metrics are equal. For hierarchical designs
(e.g. multi-layer tiles or shared-fabric modules) the instance-weighted number correctly
reflects that a sub-module instantiated N times contributes N × its own mul count.

## Reading The Report

- `*_Baseline` designs are the original direct operator implementations.
- `*_SharedFabric` designs instantiate the reusable fabric modules.
- Multiply and add counts are line-based proxies from generated SystemVerilog, not synthesis resource counts.
- Shared-fabric versions may be larger before time multiplexing because module boundaries are explicit.
